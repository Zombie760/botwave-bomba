## Tax Avoidance/Evasion: The Art of Not Paying

Trump's tax avoidance strategies were sophisticated, multi-layered, and often legally dubious. He employed income shifting, sham transactions, offshore accounts, and aggressive deductions to minimize his tax liability.

- **Income Shifting:** Trump and his family used sham corporations like All County Building Supply & Maintenance to funnel money and avoid gift taxes, inflating prices of supplies to create artificial deductions.
- **Offshore Accounts:** Trump utilized offshore entities in Panama, the Cayman Islands, and the British Virgin Islands to hide assets and avoid U.S. taxation.
- **Aggressive Deductions:** Trump deducted personal expenses such as $70,000 for hair care as business costs, pushing the boundaries of tax law.
- **Accelerated Depreciation:** The Trump tax law allowed companies to immediately write off investment costs, enabling significant tax avoidance.
- **Stock Options and Offshore Profit-Shifting:** Trump's businesses used stock option tax breaks and offshore profit-shifting to further reduce taxable income.

The result was an effective tax rate often below 1%, despite earning hundreds of millions annually. For example, Trump paid only $750 in federal income taxes in 2016 and 2017, and nothing in 10 of the years between 2001 and 2015. The IRS audited Trump for potential tax fraud, including failure to report canceled debts as income, but these audits were closed without assessment or payment of deficiencies.

This tax avoidance was not merely aggressive accounting but part of a broader strategy of financial engineering that preserved Trump's wealth while avoiding accountability.
